GDP per Capita and Educational Outcomes: Why Nations Over- and Underperform Their Income-Predicted Attainment

Development Economics • Cross-National Analysis • April 2026

This study examines why countries systematically over- or underperform their income-predicted educational attainment across 80 nations. Original OLS regression analysis finds a strong positive association between ln(GDP per capita) and adult literacy (r = 0.81, R² = 0.66). Adding institutional dummy variables — Soviet education legacy and active armed conflict — raises explained variance to 83%. Conflict-affected countries score approximately 26 percentage points lower on literacy at equivalent income levels.
